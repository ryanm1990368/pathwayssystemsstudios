Frequently Asked Questions

Everything You Need to Know

Common questions about working with Pathway Systems Studio

What types of organizations do you work with?

+

We work exclusively with small to medium-sized organizations focused on sustainable development and creating positive social impact. This includes non-profits, NGOs, social enterprises, Indigenous health organizations, community foundations, and mission-driven startups. Our ideal clients are organizations that are serious about translating their vision into measurable results and are ready to invest in strategic support.

How does your engagement process work?

+

Our process begins with a free 30-minute consultation call to understand your needs and determine if we're a good fit. If we move forward, we conduct a deep discovery phase to understand your organization, challenges, and goals. We then develop a customized proposal outlining scope, timeline, deliverables, and investment. Throughout the engagement, we work collaboratively with your team, providing regular updates and opportunities for feedback.

What are your typical project timelines?

+

Timelines vary based on project scope and complexity. Grant proposals typically take 2-4 weeks, strategic communications plans 4-6 weeks, and program evaluations 8-16 weeks. We work with you to establish realistic timelines that balance quality with your deadlines. For urgent needs, we can often accommodate expedited timelines with advance notice.

How do you structure your fees?

+

We offer flexible fee structures tailored to each project: project-based fees for defined deliverables (like grant proposals or evaluations), hourly rates for ongoing support or variable-scope work, and retainer arrangements for organizations needing consistent strategic support. We provide transparent pricing in our proposals with clear payment schedules. We're also experienced in working with organizations that have limited budgets and can discuss creative solutions.

Do you work remotely or require on-site presence?

+

We work both remotely and on-site depending on project needs and client preferences. Our remote capabilities allow us to serve organizations across Canada and internationally with full effectiveness. For projects requiring in-person presence (such as stakeholder consultations or on-site evaluations), we're available for travel within Canada and can discuss international travel for the right engagements.

What is your success rate with grant applications?

+

We have a strong track record of success, having helped secure over $1.5M in funding for clients. However, grant success depends on many factors including organizational readiness, funder priorities, competition, and alignment with funding criteria. We conduct thorough assessments before taking on grant writing projects to ensure realistic chances of success. We focus on competitive applications and strategic funder matching rather than volume.

Can you help with both strategy development AND implementation?

+

Absolutely. While some consulting firms only provide strategic recommendations, we believe in partnership through implementation. We can support you through the entire journey‚Äîfrom developing the strategy to executing it, managing projects, and measuring results. This ensures strategies don't just sit on a shelf but translate into real organizational change and impact.

What makes Pathway Systems Studio different from other consultants?

+

Three key differentiators: (1) Deep sector expertise - we specialize exclusively in sustainable development and understand the unique challenges of impact-driven work, (2) Rigorous + adaptive approach - we combine academic rigor with real-world practicality, ensuring our work is evidence-based yet highly applicable, and (3) Capacity-building focus - we don't just deliver outputs; we build your team's capabilities so you're stronger long after our engagement ends.

Do you require a minimum project size or budget?

+

We work with organizations of various sizes and budgets. While we do have minimum project thresholds to ensure we can deliver quality work, we're flexible and creative in structuring engagements. For smaller organizations or projects, we can discuss phased approaches, focused deliverables, or capacity-building workshops that provide value within budget constraints. Contact us to discuss your specific situation.

What information do you need to get started?

+

To begin, we need: (1) a clear description of the challenge or opportunity you're addressing, (2) any relevant background materials (strategic plans, past reports, funding guidelines), (3) your timeline and any key deadlines, (4) your budget range or constraints, and (5) information about stakeholders involved in decision-making. Don't worry if you don't have everything‚Äîwe'll work with you to gather what's needed during our discovery phase.
